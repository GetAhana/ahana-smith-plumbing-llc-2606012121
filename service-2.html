Water heaters

Water heater repair and replacement you can rely on

Cold showers and soggy utility closets are signs your water heater is failing. We size the right tank or tankless unit for your home, handle all connections and venting safely, dispose of the old unit, and walk you through warranty coverage before we leave.

Water heater standards we follow

  • Proper venting and combustion air per Phoenix building code
  • T&P (temperature and pressure) relief valve tested and labeled
  • Drain pan and condensate lines installed where required
  • Full flush and flush-on-demand systems for tank longevity

120+ water heaters installed and serviced across Phoenix metro—matching tank size to household demand since 2012.

How we handle Water heaters

Our water heater process takes the guesswork out of sizing and installation.

  • Tell us your hot water needs — Family size, shower frequency, and whether you want fast recovery or lower energy bills all factor in. We ask questions upfront so we size the right unit—oversized costs more to run, undersized leaves you cold.
  • Receive your options and pricing — Tank models, tankless, hybrid—we show efficiency ratings, warranty terms, and long-term costs. You decide what fits your budget and lifestyle.
  • Install, test, and haul away — New unit connected, vented, pressure-tested, and labeled. Old tank hauled away. We review the warranty booklet and answer questions before we go.
When to call us

These signs mean your water heater is nearing the end.

  • No hot water or water takes 5+ minutes to heat up
  • Rust-colored water from the heater or tank leaks at the base
  • Rumbling, popping, or sediment noise during heating cycles
  • Age over 10 years with rising energy bills and slow recovery
  • Visible corrosion, bulging, or pooling water under the tank

FAQ — Water heaters

Tank heaters are lower upfront cost and easy to maintain. Tankless saves energy and space if you accept a slight delay on first-draw hot water. We'll show you the payback timeline and let you choose based on your priorities.
Tank models average 10–12 years with regular flushing; tankless systems run 15–20 years. Hard water and sediment buildup shorten lifespan, which is why we flush tanks annually as part of maintenance.
We provide 1 year labor warranty on all water heater installs. The unit itself carries a manufacturer warranty—typically 6 years tank, 10–15 years tankless—which we review with you on the invoice and paperwork.
